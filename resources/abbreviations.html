Do Not Use List (Joint Commission)

These abbreviations are banned by The Joint Commission because they've caused medication errors and patient harm. You may still see them on older charts, but you should never write them yourself.

Don't Use Problem Use Instead
U Mistaken for "0" (zero), "4" (four), or "cc" Write "unit"
IU Mistaken for "IV" (intravenous) or "10" (ten) Write "international unit"
Q.D., QD, q.d., qd Mistaken for Q.O.D. (every other day) or "QID" Write "daily"
Q.O.D., QOD, q.o.d., qod Mistaken for Q.D. (daily) or "QID" Write "every other day"
Trailing zero (X.0 mg) Decimal point missed, 10x overdose Write "X mg" (no trailing zero)
Lack of leading zero (.X mg) Decimal point missed, 10x overdose Write "0.X mg" (use leading zero)
MS, MSO4, MgSO4 Confused for one another (morphine vs magnesium) Write "morphine sulfate" or "magnesium sulfate"
μg Mistaken for "mg" (1000x overdose) Write "mcg" or "microgram"
cc Mistaken for "U" (units) Write "mL"
AS, AD, AU Mistaken for OS, OD, OU (eye abbreviations) Write "left ear," "right ear," "both ears"
OS, OD, OU Mistaken for AS, AD, AU (ear abbreviations) Write "left eye," "right eye," "both eyes"
HS Mistaken for "half-strength" or "hour of sleep" Write "at bedtime" or "half-strength"
SC, SQ, sub q SC mistaken for SL (sublingual); SQ mistaken for "5 every" Write "subcut" or "subcutaneously"
D/C Mistaken for "discharge" or "discontinue" Write "discharge" or "discontinue"
