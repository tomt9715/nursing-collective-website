Adult Values Only

These are adult reference ranges. Pediatric values differ significantly, especially for newborns and infants. Always verify ranges with your facility's lab reference.

Complete Blood Count (CBC)

Lab Test Normal Range Critical Values Clinical Significance
WBC
White Blood Cells
 4,500-11,000/μL
>30,000 <2,000
↑ High: Infection, inflammation, leukemia, stress
↓ Low: Bone marrow suppression, immunodeficiency, chemotherapy
RBC
Red Blood Cells
 M: 4.5-5.5 million/μL
F: 4.0-5.0 million/μL
<2.0 million
↑ High: Dehydration, polycythemia, chronic hypoxia
↓ Low: Anemia, hemorrhage, bone marrow failure
Hemoglobin
Hgb / Hb
 M: 13.5-17.5 g/dL
F: 12.0-16.0 g/dL
>20 g/dL <7 g/dL
↑ High: Dehydration, polycythemia, COPD
↓ Low: Anemia, blood loss, nutritional deficiency
Hematocrit
Hct
 M: 38-50%
F: 36-44%
>60% <20%
↑ High: Dehydration, polycythemia
↓ Low: Anemia, overhydration, blood loss
Platelets
PLT / Thrombocytes
 150,000-400,000/μL
>1,000,000 <50,000
↑ High: Inflammation, malignancy, post-splenectomy
↓ Low: Bleeding risk, bone marrow suppression, DIC

Basic Metabolic Panel (BMP)

Lab Test Normal Range Critical Values Clinical Significance
Glucose
Fasting Blood Sugar
 70-100 mg/dL (fasting)
>400 mg/dL <50 mg/dL
↑ High: Diabetes, stress, steroids, infection
↓ Low: Insulin overdose, sepsis, liver failure
HbA1c
Glycated Hemoglobin
 <5.7%
>10%
↑ High: Poor glycemic control over 2-3 months
Goal: <7% for most diabetics

Electrolytes

Lab Test Normal Range Critical Values Clinical Significance
Sodium
Na+
 136-145 mEq/L
>160 mEq/L <120 mEq/L
↑ High: Dehydration, diabetes insipidus, hyperaldosteronism
↓ Low: SIADH, diuretics, heart failure, vomiting
Potassium
K+
 3.5-5.0 mEq/L
>6.5 mEq/L <2.5 mEq/L
↑ High: Renal failure, ACE inhibitors, tissue damage - CARDIAC RISK
↓ Low: Diuretics, vomiting, diarrhea - CARDIAC RISK
Chloride
Cl-
 98-106 mEq/L
>120 mEq/L <80 mEq/L
↑ High: Dehydration, metabolic acidosis
↓ Low: Vomiting, metabolic alkalosis, diuretics
Calcium
Ca++
 8.5-10.5 mg/dL
>13 mg/dL <6 mg/dL
↑ High: Hyperparathyroidism, malignancy, immobility
↓ Low: Hypoparathyroidism, vitamin D deficiency, renal failure
Magnesium
Mg++
 1.5-2.5 mEq/L
>4.0 mEq/L <1.0 mEq/L
↑ High: Renal failure, antacid overuse
↓ Low: Alcoholism, malnutrition, diuretics - affects K+ and Ca++
Phosphorus
PO4
 2.5-4.5 mg/dL
>8.0 mg/dL <1.0 mg/dL
↑ High: Renal failure, hypoparathyroidism
↓ Low: Malnutrition, alcoholism, antacids

Renal Function

Lab Test Normal Range Critical Values Clinical Significance
BUN
Blood Urea Nitrogen
 7-20 mg/dL
>100 mg/dL
↑ High: Dehydration, renal failure, GI bleeding, high protein diet
↓ Low: Liver failure, malnutrition, overhydration
Creatinine
Cr
 M: 0.7-1.3 mg/dL
F: 0.6-1.1 mg/dL
>10 mg/dL
↑ High: Renal impairment (more specific than BUN)
↓ Low: Low muscle mass, pregnancy
GFR
Glomerular Filtration Rate
 >90 mL/min/1.73m²
<15 (Stage 5 CKD)
↓ Low: CKD staging: 60-89 (Stage 2), 30-59 (Stage 3), 15-29 (Stage 4), <15 (Stage 5/ESRD)

Cardiac Markers

Lab Test Normal Range Critical Values Clinical Significance
Troponin I
cTnI
 <0.04 ng/mL
>0.4 ng/mL
↑ High: MI (rises 3-6 hrs, peaks 12-24 hrs), myocarditis, PE
BNP
B-type Natriuretic Peptide
 <100 pg/mL
>400 pg/mL
↑ High: Heart failure (higher = worse HF), renal failure can also elevate
CK-MB

 <5% of total CK
>5% of total CK
↑ High: MI (rises 4-8 hrs, returns to normal 48-72 hrs), cardiac surgery

Coagulation Studies

Lab Test Normal Range Critical Values Clinical Significance
PT
Prothrombin Time
 11-13 seconds
>20 seconds
↑ Prolonged: Warfarin therapy, liver disease, vitamin K deficiency
INR
International Normalized Ratio
 0.9-1.1
>4.0
Therapeutic: 2.0-3.0 for most on warfarin, 2.5-3.5 for mechanical valves
↑ High: Bleeding risk, hold warfarin, consider vitamin K
PTT / aPTT
Partial Thromboplastin Time
 25-35 seconds
>100 seconds
Therapeutic: 1.5-2.5x normal for heparin therapy
↑ Prolonged: Heparin, hemophilia, DIC
D-dimer
Fibrin Degradation Product
 <0.5 μg/mL
Varies by assay
↑ High: DVT, PE, DIC, recent surgery, pregnancy (negative rules out clot)

Thyroid Function

Lab Test Normal Range Critical Values Clinical Significance
TSH
Thyroid Stimulating Hormone
 0.4-4.0 mU/L
>20 mU/L <0.1 mU/L
↑ High: Hypothyroidism (thyroid underactive, body makes more TSH)
↓ Low: Hyperthyroidism (thyroid overactive, body suppresses TSH)
Free T4
Free Thyroxine
 0.8-1.8 ng/dL
>5.0 ng/dL <0.4 ng/dL
↑ High: Hyperthyroidism, thyroiditis
↓ Low: Hypothyroidism
Free T3
Free Triiodothyronine
 2.3-4.2 pg/mL
>8.0 pg/mL
↑ High: Hyperthyroidism, T3 toxicosis
↓ Low: Hypothyroidism, severe illness
