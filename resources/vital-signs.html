Quick Concepts

Younger = Faster Infants and children have higher HR and RR than adults. This is normal.
BP Increases with Age Normal BP is lower in children. Adult BP norms don't apply to kids.
Trends Matter Most A single reading is less important than changes over time.
Context is Everything Crying, fever, pain, and activity all affect vitals. Consider the whole picture.

When to be concerned: Sustained rates outside these ranges, irregular rhythms, or rates that don't match the clinical picture (e.g., bradycardia in a crying infant). Athletes may have lower resting HR (40-60) which can be normal.

Look for work of breathing: Retractions (intercostal, subcostal, suprasternal), nasal flaring, head bobbing (infants), grunting, use of accessory muscles. These signs of respiratory distress are often more important than the rate alone.

Adult BP categories: Normal (<120/<80), Elevated (120-129/<80), Stage 1 HTN (130-139 or 80-89), Stage 2 HTN (≥140 or ≥90), Hypertensive Crisis (>180 and/or >120 - requires immediate attention). For kids, use age/height percentile charts.

Fever definition: Generally ≥100.4°F (38°C). In neonates <28 days, ANY fever is an emergency - notify provider immediately. Hypothermia (<95°F/35°C) also requires urgent attention. Elderly patients may not mount a fever even with serious infection.
